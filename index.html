PROFILE

FROM "THE VALLEY" TO SILICON VALLEY

CREATIVE AND CAPABLE FULL STACK DEVELOPER

My name is Stephanie Provence and I'm starting a new career as a web designer/software developer. After graduating from CSU Monterey Bay with a degree in Cinema Arts, I moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in editing in the TV/Film industry. After 10 years, I moved back to Sacramento and decided a career change was in order.

At first I was only interested in Front End Development, but I've come to appreciate merging the front and back together to create the full stack. Every day I learn something new and am excited to apply these new skills and ideas to whatever project I'm currently working on.

My time in the film industry gave me a lot of experiences that I believe directly apply to web design/development. I have a lot of experience working with clients and customizing each project to fit specific needs. I am very creative and can bring outside the box ideas to everything I work on. I am accustomed to working in highly collaborative environments where each person plays a specific role in completing the finished product. My past experiences plus my ever-growing coding knowledge make me the perfect asset to any project.

PROJECTS

Rapid Logger / July 2019

The brain often doesn't work in an orderly fashion so organizing your task manager that way doesn't always work. The Rapid Logger is designed to be a personal task management system that helps you capture and organize your tasks/events/thoughts quickly and easily. Simply add text to the input box using our hotkeys and we sort it for you! Tools Used: HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, Materialize, MySQL, Sequelize, Express, Node, Passport.js, Handlebars

Friend Finder / June 2019

A friend finding app that takes user responses to a quick survey and stores them in a database. Their answers are then compared to the other users in the database and the person with the closest results to their answers are returned as their match. Tools Used: HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, JavaScript, jQuery, Node.js, and Express.js.

TRVL / May 2019

A one-stop-shop travel website where users can search a variety of destinations with high quality photos, weather information, and a map with information specific to the city. Users can also compare their current search with previous searches. Tools Used: HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, jQuery, Firebase, Bootstrap
